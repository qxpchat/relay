# Privacy

`{{ config.mail_domain }}` runs a [chatmail](https://chatmail.at) relay — a minimal email server purpose-built for end-to-end-encrypted messaging. Chatmail relays do not, by design, collect personal data: no signup, no phone number, no real name, no profile information ever leaves your device.

Because there is no personal-data processing, a traditional privacy policy is not strictly required. This page documents what the relay actually does, so you can verify it.

## What we don't have

- **Your name, address, phone number.** Never collected, never asked.
- **Your contacts.** Kept on your device, never on the server.
- **Message content.** Every message is end-to-end encrypted before it reaches the relay. We can't read it, decrypt it, or hand it over.
- **Read receipts, location, presence, contact graphs, group memberships.** Not collected, not synced, not derivable from anything we hold.

## What we do have, briefly

- **In-transit messages.** Encrypted ciphertext is stored only until the recipient picks it up, and removed at the latest after {{ config.delete_mails_after }} days regardless. Sooner if storage pressure requires it.
- **Account credentials.** A random `<random>@{{ config.mail_domain }}` address and a server-side password hash. The address is generated by us at signup; we have no link between it and your real-world identity.
- **Server access logs.** `nginx`, `postfix`, and `dovecot` keep short-lived operational logs (typically a few days) used for debugging and abuse prevention. These contain IP addresses by necessity — TCP requires it.
- **Inactive accounts** are removed entirely after {{ config.delete_inactive_users_after }} days of no successful login. Mail and credentials go with the account.

## Source code

The relay software is open source. Our fork lives at [github.com/qxpchat/relay](https://github.com/qxpchat/relay); upstream chatmail is at [github.com/chatmail/relay](https://github.com/chatmail/relay). Anyone can audit what the server does.

## Account deletion

Remove the `{{ config.mail_domain }}` profile from your qxp app on every device you use it on. The server-side account is then automatically deleted after {{ config.delete_inactive_users_after }} days.

## Caveats

This page describes practice, not legal positioning. The qxp project is not (yet) operating under a formal data-protection structure with a registered data-protection officer or named supervisory authority. If you have legal or regulatory requirements that depend on a formal privacy notice (commercial, governmental, healthcare, …), please use a different relay until we publish a fuller policy.
